## Route 21

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/3/3b/Kanto_Route_21_FRLG.png/100px-Kanto_Route_21_FRLG.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Kanto_Route_21_FRLG.png) Route 21

[Route 21](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Route_21 "Kanto Route 21") is an ocean route that leads from [Cinnabar Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Cinnabar_Island "Cinnabar Island") back to [Pallet Town](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pallet_Town "Pallet Town"). A single patch of tall grass grows just outside of town.

## Pallet Town

Having now explored nearly all of the [Kanto](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto "Kanto") region, it is a good time to tie up any loose ends, the most important of which is the [Power Plant](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Power_Plant "Kanto Power Plant"). To get there, fly back to [Route 10](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Route_10 "Kanto Route 10").

## Route 10

To reach the [Power Plant](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Power_Plant "Kanto Power Plant"), use [Surf](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Surf_(move) "Surf (move)") to follow the river southward past the [Rock Tunnel](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_Tunnel "Rock Tunnel") entrance. It is only a short distance away, with a single PokéManiac outside the building.

#### In Search of Power

Pass through the entrance room to get a [Max Potion](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Max_Potion "Max Potion"). Continue in a clockwise direction to the large, central room to find [TM17](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM17 "TM17") ( [Protect](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Protect_(move) "Protect (move)")) and an [Elixir](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Elixir "Elixir"). Collect [TM25](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM25 "TM25") ( [Thunder](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Thunder_(move) "Thunder (move)")) in the southeast room, then follow the narrow hallway northward. Grab the [ThunderStone](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Thunder_Stone "Thunder Stone") from the northeast corner of the northeast room. Continue westward to reach the final room.

##### VS Zapdos

[Zapdos](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Zapdos_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Zapdos (Pokémon)") is the last of the [legendary birds](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Legendary_birds "Legendary birds") that you will encounter. Use [Rock](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_(type) "Rock (type)")\- and [Ice-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ice_(type) "Ice (type)") attacks to weaken it quickly, then follow up with [Grass](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grass_(type) "Grass (type)"), [Flying](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Flying_(type) "Flying (type)"), and [Fighting](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)") moves to wear its health down further. Like with [Articuno](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Articuno_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Articuno (Pokémon)") and [Moltres](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Moltres_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Moltres (Pokémon)"), it will be useful to cause status ailments like [Sleep](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sleep_(status_condition) "Sleep (status condition)") to increase the odds of catching it.

### Viridian Gym

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/2/25/Viridian_Gym_FRLG.png/300px-Viridian_Gym_FRLG.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Viridian_Gym_FRLG.png) Viridian Gym

**VIRIDIAN CITY POKéMON GYM**

...

In the time it took to collect the seven other Badges, the Gym Leader of [Viridian Gym](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Viridian_Gym "Viridian Gym") has finally returned. [Giovanni](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giovanni "Giovanni") resumed his position as Gym Leader shortly after his defeat in [Saffron City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Saffron_City "Saffron City"). The [Viridian Gym](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Viridian_Gym "Viridian Gym") specializes in [Ground-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ground_(type) "Ground (type)") Pokémon. [Grass](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grass_(type) "Grass (type)")-, [Water](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Water_(type) "Water (type)")-, and [Ice-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ice_(type) "Ice (type)") attacks will be the most useful. [Flying-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Flying_(type) "Flying (type)") Pokémon are immune to Ground attacks and Flying moves will deal serious damage to the several [Fighting](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)") Pokémon inside. [Psychic](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Psychic_(type) "Psychic (type)") moves will work wonders against [Poison-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Poison_(type) "Poison (type)") Pokémon.

Like the [Rocket Hideout](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Team_Rocket_Hideout "Team Rocket Hideout") in [Celadon City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celadon_City "Celadon City"), the Viridian Gym also employs [spinner tiles](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Spinner_tile "Spinner tile") to confuse and disorient any challengers. Go north through the center room. Battle Ace Trainer Warren and Black Belt Takashi, so that Takashi walks away from the north wall. Return to the entrance and follow the spinner tiles north and west to reach the Gym Leader.

After the battle, [Giovanni](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giovanni "Giovanni") awards you the [Earth Badge](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Badge#Earth_Badge "Badge"), which ensures obedience from all Pokémon, regardless of level. As a prize, he also gives you [TM26](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM26 "TM26") ( [Earthquake](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Earthquake_(move) "Earthquake (move)")), a TM he claims to have made when he first acted as Gym Leader. He seems remorseful of his actions, and officially disbands [Team Rocket](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Team_Rocket "Team Rocket") before disappearing.

### The Path to the Summit

Having won all eight Gym Badges, the next destination is the [Indigo Plateau](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Indigo_Plateau "Indigo Plateau"), far to the northwest. The elite Trainers there are tough, but before you can challenge them, you must first survive a grueling cave known as [Victory Road](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Victory_Road_(Kanto) "Victory Road (Kanto)"). Gather your strongest Pokémon team with access to [Surf](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Surf_(move) "Surf (move)") and [Strength](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Strength_(move) "Strength (move)"), and visit a Poké Mart to replenish your supply of Potions and Poké Balls.
