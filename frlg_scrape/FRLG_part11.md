Saffron City is a sprawling metropolis located in the heart of the region between [Cerulean City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Cerulean_City "Cerulean City"), [Celadon City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celadon_City "Celadon City"), [Lavender Town](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lavender_Town "Lavender Town"), and [Vermilion City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Vermilion_City "Vermilion City"). The [Silph Co.](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Silph_Co. "Silph Co.") headquarters dominates the city skyline, and two Pokémon Gyms stand in the northeast district. It is the most populous city in all of [Kanto](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto "Kanto").

### Mr. Psychic's house

The southeast house belongs to a man known as Mr. Psychic. Speak to him and he claims to know exactly why you are here, and gives you [TM29](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM29 "TM29") ( [Psychic](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Psychic_(move) "Psychic (move)")).

### Silph Co.

The [Silph Co](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Silph_Co "Silph Co"). is the leading manufacturer of items like Poké Balls, Potions, and TMs. Their products are supplied to every Poké Mart in the region. The company has been a target of [Team Rocket](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Team_Rocket "Team Rocket"), first for their [Silph Scope](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Silph_Scope "Silph Scope") prototype, and now for a top-secret development project. The Rocket Grunt posted outside the building has fallen asleep, leaving a perfect opportunity to sneak inside. The building holds eleven floors, with [warp panels](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Warp_panel "Warp panel") allowing the workers to quickly move around. This convenience ends up acting as a maze for anyone trying to reach the top floor.

#### 2F

It is not possible to open the electronic doors yet, so step on either [warp panel](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Warp_panel "Warp panel") to teleport to the eighth floor.

#### 8F

Battle Scientist Parker and the two Rocket Grunts, then grab the [Iron](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Iron "Iron") in the eastern room. Take the elevator down to 3F.

#### 3F

Take the northeast warp panel back to the second floor. Speak to the woman here in the northwest room, and this [Move Tutor](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Move_Tutor "Move Tutor") offers to teach [Thunder Wave](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Thunder_Wave_(move) "Thunder Wave (move)") to one of your Pokémon. This can be done at any time, even after clearing the building. Warp back to 3F.

Fight the Rocket Grunt and take the warp panel in the southwest to reach 5F; pick up the [TM01](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM01 "TM01") ( [Focus Punch](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Focus_Punch_(move) "Focus Punch (move)")) from the southwest room before returning to 3F.

Climb up the stairs to 4F.

#### 4F

Battle another two Rocket Grunts, pick up [TM41](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM41 "TM41") ( [Torment](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Torment_(move) "Torment (move)")) in the eastern room, and climb to the fifth floor.

#### 5F

Fight the Rocket Grunt near the stairs and Scientist Beau to the west. Follow the hallway southward to reach another Rocket Grunt standing next to a warp panel. Defeat him, step on the warp panel, then warp right back. Turn eastward to find a [Card Key](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Card_Key_(Kanto) "Card Key (Kanto)"), which is used to open the building's electronic doors. Pass by the warp panel again and unlock the northwest room to reach a [Protein](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Protein "Protein"). Take the stairs up to 6F.

#### 6F

Follow the western hallway past Scientist Taylor and unlock the southwest room to get an [HP Up](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/HP_Up "HP Up") and an [X Special](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/X_Sp._Atk "X Sp. Atk"). Climb the stairs to 7F.

#### 7F

Use the Card Key to unlock the east-central room and pick up [TM08](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM08 "TM08") ( [Bulk Up](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bulk_Up_(move) "Bulk Up (move)")). Turn around and follow the central hallway to the southwest room. Battle Scientist Joshua and grab the [Calcium](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Calcium "Calcium") before taking the stairs up to 9F.

#### 9F

Defeat the Rocket Grunt at the south end of the central hallway, then open the door to reach the break room. Speak to the woman here, and she heals your Pokémon to full health as thanks for your efforts. Climb the stairs to the tenth floor.

#### 10F

Battle Scientist Travis and the Rocket Grunt, then pick up the [Carbos](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Carbos "Carbos") and [Rare Candy](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rare_Candy "Rare Candy") from the southwest room. Ride the elevator all the way down to 3F.

#### 3F

Use the Card Key to unlock the central room. Open the door to the west-central room beyond that, defeat Scientist Jose, and grab the nearby [Hyper Potion](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hyper_Potion "Hyper Potion"). Take the warp in the central room.

#### 7F (Northwest)

When you reach the northwest room on the seventh floor, [Blue](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Blue_(game) "Blue (game)") is waiting for you.

##### Rival Battle 6

#### 11F (West)

Take out one last Rocket Grunt to reach the boardroom. Use the Card Key to open the door and confront [Giovanni](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giovanni "Giovanni"). He claims to be having some important business meeting with the company president and warns you to stay out of grown-up matters before drawing you into battle.

##### VS Giovanni

[Giovanni](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giovanni "Giovanni")'s [Nidorino](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Nidorino_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Nidorino (Pokémon)"), [Rhyhorn](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rhyhorn_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Rhyhorn (Pokémon)"), and [Nidoqueen](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Nidoqueen_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Nidoqueen (Pokémon)") all take serious damage from [Ground-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ground_(type) "Ground (type)") attacks like [Dig](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Dig_(move) "Dig (move)") and [Earthquake](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Earthquake_(move) "Earthquake (move)"). The latter two are also vulnerable to [Water](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Water_(type) "Water (type)") moves. His [Kangaskhan](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kangaskhan_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Kangaskhan (Pokémon)") only takes super effective damage from [Fighting](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)") attacks.

### Copycat's house

#### Move Tutor

Now that the Rockets have left the city, the Grunt outside the northwest house is gone. A girl inside known as the [Copycat](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Copycat_(character) "Copycat (character)") likes to collect [Pokémon dolls](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_doll "Pokémon doll"). Give her a [Poké Doll](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9_Doll "Poké Doll"), and she offers to [teach](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Move_Tutor "Move Tutor") the move [Mimic](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Mimic_(move) "Mimic (move)") to a compatible Pokémon.

### Fighting Dojo

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/8/83/Fighting_Dojo_FRLG.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Fighting_Dojo_FRLG.png) Fighting Dojo

[Saffron City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Saffron_City "Saffron City") is unique as the only city to have two Pokémon Gyms. The [Fighting Dojo](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_Dojo "Fighting Dojo") is located in the northeast neighborhood, next door to the [Saffron Gym](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Saffron_Gym "Saffron Gym"). The two clubs had once competed to determine which would become the city's one official Gym, and the [Psychic](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Psychic_(type) "Psychic (type)") Pokémon of Saffron Gym were victorious. There is no Badge to be won here, but defeat the [Karate Master](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Koichi "Koichi") to earn the choice of two rare [Fighting-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)") Pokémon: the hard-kicking [Hitmonlee](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hitmonlee_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Hitmonlee (Pokémon)") or the piston-punching [Hitmonchan](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hitmonchan_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Hitmonchan (Pokémon)"). Use Psychic- and [Flying-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Flying_(type) "Flying (type)") attacks here for greatest effect.

### Saffron Gym

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/a/a7/Saffron_Gym_FRLG.png/300px-Saffron_Gym_FRLG.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Saffron_Gym_FRLG.png) Saffron Gym

**SAFFRON CITY POKéMON GYM**

**LEADER: SABRINA**

The Master of Psychic Pokémon!

The [Saffron Gym](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Saffron_Gym "Saffron Gym") specializes in [Psychic](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Psychic_(type) "Psychic (type)") Pokémon. Psychic-type moves are super effective against [Fighting](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)")\- and [Poison-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Poison_(type) "Poison (type)") Pokémon, while Psychic-type Pokémon are weak to [Bug](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bug_(type) "Bug (type)")-, [Dark](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Dark_(type) "Dark (type)")-, and [Ghost-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ghost_(type) "Ghost (type)") moves.

The Gym uses [a series of warp panels](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Saffron_Gym#Generations_I-IV "Saffron Gym") to confuse and disorient any challengers. Step on the panel near the entrance to reach the southeast room. To reach the Gym Leader by using as few warps as possible, continue by stepping on the northwest, southwest, and southwest panels in the following rooms. This leads to the center room, where [Sabrina](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sabrina "Sabrina") awaits anyone up to the challenge.

After the battle, [Sabrina](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sabrina "Sabrina") awards you the [Marsh Badge](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Badge#Marsh_Badge "Badge"), which ensures obedience from all Pokémon up to level 70 and enables the use of [Rock Smash](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_Smash_(move) "Rock Smash (move)") in the field. She also gives you [TM04](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/TM04 "TM04") ( [Calm Mind](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Calm_Mind_(move) "Calm Mind (move)")) as a prize.

The next destination is [Fuchsia City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fuchsia_City "Fuchsia City") to the south. There are two paths that lead there: either take Cycling Road west of [Celadon City](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celadon_City "Celadon City"), or follow the coast south of [Lavender Town](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lavender_Town "Lavender Town").
