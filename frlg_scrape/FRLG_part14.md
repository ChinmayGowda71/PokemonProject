### One Island (Town)

#### Pokémon Network Center

Upon reaching [One Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/One_Island_Town "One Island Town"), [Bill](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bill "Bill") leads you into the [Pokémon Network Center](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Network_Center "Pokémon Network Center") and introduces you to his friend [Celio](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celio "Celio"), a programmer who runs the PC network for the [Sevii Islands](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sevii_Islands "Sevii Islands"). The building functions as a traditional Pokémon Center and also houses the Pokémon Network Machine. Celio is currently performing maintenance on the machine, but the islands are too remote and the machine is having trouble connecting to Bill's PC system on the [Kanto](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto "Kanto") mainland. Until maintenance is complete, the normal PC services will be unavailable.

#### Deliver the Meteorite

While he and Celio get to work on the machine, Bill asks you a favor: visit nearby [Two Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Two_Island "Two Island") and speak to the man who runs the Game Corner there. He has an interest in rare rocks and gems, and Bill has a [Meteorite](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Meteorite "Meteorite") for you to deliver to him. To help with this task, Celio gives you a [Tri-Pass](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Tri-Pass "Tri-Pass"), a ferry ticket that enables travel between the three northernmost islands in the archipelago. He also gives you a second page for the [Town Map](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Town_Map "Town Map") that displays [One Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/One_Island "One Island"), [Two Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Two_Island "Two Island"), and [Three Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Island "Three Island").

### Treasure Beach

South of the port is a small island, with only a single Trainer swimming nearby. This is [Treasure Beach](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Treasure_Beach "Treasure Beach"), known for the valuable items that regularly wash up on the shore. The tides usually replace these items by the next day, allowing treasure hunters to easily stockpile these rare items.

### Kindle Road

[Kindle Road](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kindle_Road "Kindle Road"), on the island's rocky eastern side, leads northward from the town to [Mt. Ember](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Mt._Ember "Mt. Ember"). The grassy western path is packed with Trainers, and is the only location where wild [Ponyta](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ponyta_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Ponyta (Pokémon)") and [Rapidash](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rapidash_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Rapidash (Pokémon)") can be found. A rocky area on the northern edge of the main island houses the [Ember Spa](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ember_Spa "Ember Spa").

#### Ember Spa

Ember Spa is a small hot spring. By standing in the middle of the water, people and Pokémon can relax and restore their health.

##### Mind Over Matter

The old man standing between the two waterfalls claims to have carved the cave out of a gigantic boulder many years ago. As he no longer needs the tool he used, he gives the [HM06](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/HM06 "HM06") ( [Rock Smash](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_Smash_(move) "Rock Smash (move)")) to you. This field move allows you to remove [weak-looking rocks](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Breakable_rock "Breakable rock") to clear a path. [Certain Pokémon](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Breakable_rock#Pok%C3%A9mon_FireRed_and_LeafGreen "Breakable rock") like to hide themselves under these rocks, and will attack when their hiding place is removed!

#### Mt. Ember

[Mt. Ember](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Mt._Ember "Mt. Ember") is a dormant volcano located at the northernmost point of [One Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/One_Island "One Island"), across a small channel from Ember Spa.

##### Exterior

To the east, a pair of [Team Rocket](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Team_Rocket "Team Rocket") Grunts are whispering to themselves about digging and treasure. Near the entrance, a Hiker offers to teach [Explosion](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Explosion_(move) "Explosion (move)") to a compatible Pokémon. Go west and use [Strength](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Strength_(move) "Strength (move)") to move a pair of boulders, then battle Pokémon Ranger Beth. Head north from the second patch of tall grass to battle the jogging Crush Girl Jocelyn, then go east to find a [Dire Hit](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Dire_Hit "Dire Hit"). Turn around and go west from Jocelyn to fight Pokémon Ranger Logan and venture inside the mountain.

##### Interior

Follow the tunnels eastward through the volcano to re-emerge on the upper east side.

##### Exterior

Climb up the first stairway to find a group of boulders and [cracked rocks](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Breakable_rock "Breakable rock") to the east. Use [Rock Smash](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Rock_Smash_(move) "Rock Smash (move)") to remove the western rock, then use [Strength](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Strength_(move) "Strength (move)") to move the southernmost boulder; clear the next rock and boulder to reach a [Fire Stone](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fire_Stone "Fire Stone") on the ledge beyond. The stairway to the west leads to another [Ultra Ball](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Ultra_Ball "Ultra Ball").

##### Summit

A second group of boulders litters the area. Use [Strength](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Strength_(move) "Strength (move)") to clear a way northward to find a large bird Pokémon roosting atop the volcano.

###### VS Moltres

Moltres is the next member of the [legendary birds](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Legendary_birds "Legendary birds"). Use [Water](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Water_(type) "Water (type)")\- and [Electric-type](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Electric_(type) "Electric (type)") attacks to weaken it quickly, then follow up with [Grass](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grass_(type) "Grass (type)") and [Fighting](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fighting_(type) "Fighting (type)") moves to wear its health down further. Like with [Articuno](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Articuno_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Articuno (Pokémon)"), inflicting status ailments like [Sleep](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Sleep_(status_condition) "Sleep (status condition)") and [Paralysis](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Paralysis_(status_condition) "Paralysis (status condition)") will help increase the chances of capturing it.

## Two Island

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/6/6f/Boon_Island_FRLG.png/300px-Boon_Island_FRLG.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Boon_Island_FRLG.png) Two Island

[Two Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Two_Island "Two Island") is a tiny island with little more than a Pokémon Center. The [Joyful Game Corner](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Joyful_Game_Corner "Joyful Game Corner") is located here, and the Market Stall offers different items over time. Unlike in the town of [One Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/One_Island_(town) "One Island (town)"), the waters around the port are too rough, making it impossible to surf or fish.

#### Move Maniac

The house to the south of the Market Stall belongs to the [Move Maniac](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Move_Reminder "Move Reminder"). He claims to know every single move a Pokémon can learn as it grows up, and can re-teach any move that a Pokémon had forgotten. As a mushroom enthusiast, he accepts either two [TinyMushrooms](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Tiny_Mushroom "Tiny Mushroom") or one [Big Mushroom](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Big_Mushroom "Big Mushroom") per transaction as payment.

#### Joyful Game Corner

The [Joyful Game Corner](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Joyful_Game_Corner "Joyful Game Corner") is located on the east side of town. When you enter, you find the owner pacing around the building. It seems that his daughter, [Lostelle](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lostelle "Lostelle"), likes to bring lunch for him every day, but he is starting to worry as she is running late. He pleads with you to search [Three Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Island "Three Island"). A Biker suddenly enters the building, mistaking the area for Three Island. The owner chases him out, and asks again that you save Lostelle.

### Cape Brink

[Cape Brink](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Cape_Brink "Cape Brink") is a short mountain road on the island's north side. The lone house here belongs to a Channeler who has perfected the ultimate [Grass](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grass_(type) "Grass (type)"), [Fire](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Fire_(type) "Fire (type)"), and [Water](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Water_(type) "Water (type)") moves. She offers to teach [Frenzy Plant](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Frenzy_Plant_(move) "Frenzy Plant (move)") to [Venusaur](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Venusaur_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Venusaur (Pokémon)"), [Blast Burn](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Blast_Burn_(move) "Blast Burn (move)") to [Charizard](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Charizard_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Charizard (Pokémon)"), and [Hydro Cannon](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hydro_Cannon_(move) "Hydro Cannon (move)") to [Blastoise](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Blastoise_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Blastoise (Pokémon)"). Each of these attacks may only be taught once each, and only to your original [partner Pokémon](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/First_partner_Pok%C3%A9mon "First partner Pokémon").

## Three Island

[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/5/5a/Kin_Island.png/450px-Kin_Island.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Kin_Island.png) Three Island[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/b/b1/Berry_Forest.png/300px-Berry_Forest.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Berry_Forest.png) Berry Forest[![](https://archives.bulbagarden.net/media/upload/thumb/b/b7/Berry_Forest_berries.png/300px-Berry_Forest_berries.png)](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/File:Berry_Forest_berries.png) Berry Forest, with Berry locations

[Three Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Island "Three Island") consists of two plots of land: a larger island to the east that holds the town of [Three Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Island_(town) "Three Island (town)"), and a smaller wooded islet to the west where the [Berry Forest](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Berry_Forest "Berry Forest") is located.

### Three Isle Port

[Three Isle Port](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Isle_Port "Three Isle Port") is located on the south side of the island, connecting the port to the town of [Three Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Island_(town) "Three Island (town)"). A small cave leads into the cliffside to the east.

#### Trouble in Paradise

Upon leaving the port, you find several rowdy Bikers harassing the residents. That must mean trouble for the town, as well!

#### Three Isle Path

[Three Isle Path](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Three_Isle_Path "Three Isle Path") is a small cave on the southeast side of the larger island. The cliffside here is rumored to hide a large deposit of gold, and one of the residents mines tirelessly for the precious metal.

#### Network Machine: Online

By the time you arrive in town, [Bill](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bill "Bill") and [Celio](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celio "Celio")'s work on the Network Machine has successfully linked the [Pokémon Network Center](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Network_Center "Pokémon Network Center") with Bill's PC system on [the mainland](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto "Kanto"), restoring all normal PC functions in the process.

#### Biker, Begone!

Several more Bikers can be found causing trouble near the Pokémon Center. Approach the gang and one of them asks if you really want to take them on. Defeat the [Kanto Rider Federation](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Kanto_Pok%C3%A9mon_Federation "Kanto Pokémon Federation") in four consecutive battles and they reluctantly leave the island in peace.

#### Lostelle's house

The house with the red roof in the southwest part of town belongs to [Lostelle](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lostelle "Lostelle") and her father. She is still nowhere to be found, so she must have gone deeper into the island. With the Bikers gone, one of the residents informs you that she had gone west toward [Bond Bridge](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bond_Bridge "Bond Bridge").

### Bond Bridge

[Bond Bridge](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bond_Bridge "Bond Bridge") connects the main island with the western islet and the [Berry Forest](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Berry_Forest "Berry Forest"). Several Trainers can be found here, and valuable items sometimes wash up near the bridge.

#### Save Lostelle!

Make your way to the northwest corner of the forest to find [Lostelle](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lostelle "Lostelle"). She is terrified by a Pokémon that she saw a short time ago and is too scared to move. Just then, she starts screaming when she sees the Pokémon reappear. Defeat or capture the wild [Hypno](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Hypno_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Hypno (Pokémon)")!

Afterward, she reveals that she visited the forest to pick Berries to include with her father's lunch. As thanks for saving her, she gives you an [Iapapa Berry](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Iapapa_Berry "Iapapa Berry"). She is still scared to travel alone, so the two of you automatically travel together back to the Game Corner on [Two Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Two_Island "Two Island").

## Two Island

### Joyful Game Corner

Now that [Lostelle](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Lostelle "Lostelle") is safe and sound, speak to her father to finally deliver [Bill](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bill "Bill")'s [Meteorite](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Meteorite "Meteorite"). He gives you a [Moon Stone](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Moon_Stone "Moon Stone") as thanks, and promises to get the Game Corner back up and running right away.

#### Skip & Chomp!

The Joyful Game Corner offers two multiplayer minigames. Both games feature a selection of Berries as possible prizes.

- In [Pokémon Jump](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Jump "Pokémon Jump"), a entrant's Pokémon must jump over a [Venusaur](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Venusaur_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Venusaur (Pokémon)")'s [Vine Whip](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Vine_Whip_(move) "Vine Whip (move)") like a jumprope in sync with others to earn points within a time limit. Only small Pokémon capable of jumping are allowed to play.
- In [Dodrio Berry Picking](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Dodrio_Berry_Picking "Dodrio Berry Picking"), several [Dodrio](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Dodrio_(Pok%C3%A9mon) "Dodrio (Pokémon)") line up and try to catch as many falling Berries as possible before the timer runs out. Each entrant must have a Dodrio in their party to participate.

These games may be played with other Trainers in [FireRed, LeafGreen](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_FireRed_and_LeafGreen_Versions "Pokémon FireRed and LeafGreen Versions") and [Emerald](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Emerald_Version "Pokémon Emerald Version") Versions.

## One Island

### One Island (Town)

#### Pokémon Network Center

[Bill](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Bill "Bill") and [Celio](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Celio "Celio") have now finished their work on the Pokémon Network Machine, so Bill is ready to head home. He leads you out of the building and the two of you automatically take the ferry back to [Cinnabar Island](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Cinnabar_Island "Cinnabar Island").
